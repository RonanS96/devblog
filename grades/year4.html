HONS Project: Deliverable 1

An Online Tool to Choose a Programming Language. The first part of my honours project involved writing a research report which included a literature review, some background research on similar products, designing the product I planned on creating, carrying out some project planning and running a survey to find out the most popular programming languages. This was worth 15% of my overall grade for the project.

Biologically Inspired Computation

Year 4, Semester 1. Grade: B (66%)
66%

This module gave me an insight into some of the software that is around that is based on different biological systems. This is an alternative to traditional ways of developing software and gave me interesting experience in working with large datasets. The first coursework for this module involved building an artificial neural network and an evolutionary algorithm.

55%

This course covered a number of important concept and techniques used in data mining and machine learning. We analysed datasets and carried out supervised and unsupervised learning using an open source Java tool called WEKA.

Computing in the Classroom

Year 4, Semesters 1 & 2. Grade: A (82%)
82%

This course runs under the Undergraduate Ambassadors Scheme and provides an opportunity for students to act as ambassadors for their disciplines. I worked in a higher and a BGE (Broad General Education) classroom at West Calder High School (West Lothian) as a teaching assistant for a total of 37 hours. I also carried out a personal project on this course which involved me running three classes on my own and teaching the third year BGE class how to create mobile apps using an online tool called Thunkable.

Big Data Management

Year 4, Semester 2. Grade: A (88%)
88%

In this module we were taught exactly what is meant by 'Big Data' and worked practically with a number of different systems that are in place for dealing with Big Data. The first part of the course was focused on NoSQL and NewSQL mostly and the second part gave us an opportunity to explore Linked Data, the Semantic Web, RDF and SPARQL.
